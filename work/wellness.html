A Chance For Sincerity

Rebuilding the relationship between
USC administration and students
project info
when
3 weeks, fall 2020
role
Web Design / UI/UX
overview
This Mindful USC Wellness Check-in redesign is part of our Chance for Sincerity initiative for USC President Carol Folt. Mental health is at an all-time low due to the pandemic, but USC has yet to respond and support their students appropriately. I explored beyond the brief and created a digital solution to break down barriers that keep USC students from accessing mental health support.

More than half of USC students agree that currrent mental health services provided by USC are inadequate. This enhancement of Mindful USC ensures students get help tailored to their needs and feel supported by their administration.

Learn more about the project
challenge

What are we trying to solve?

How might we design an initiative that restores the trust between administration and students of the Trojan Family?
project scope

Converging and diverging

Our initial scope was to design signage and environmental graphics welcoming students back for the upcoming spring semester. Our focus shifted to convincing administration to create a campaign based on accountability, rebuilding the relationship between USC administration and students.

I went beyond the brief to explore technological solutions to the struggles students are currently facing, and lay the foundation for a future shaped by both students and faculty.

“I have not heard of a single mental health initiative to encourage students to seek extra support during this time.”

“USC has a misguided response to testimonies of racism and chooses the wrong battles while ignoring larger ones.” 

“The USC administration presents an image of listening to the concerns of students when it really is trying to preserve the interests of wealthy donors.”

foundational research

User Insights

Our team published a self-report survey (160) to gain a basic understanding of the climate. After conducting in-depth interviews with other USC students, we learned that the solution to rebuilding trust isn't as easy as a gratifying message.

The shocking findings validated that the administration is either unaware or negligent to how students are struggling.
define

How we approached the problem

We identified several key opportunities for the administration to address. Ultimately, the relationship between students and the administration has been severely strained.

We listened to the student body to understand the breadth of issues they were struggling with, and designed a speculative mockup series of posters, banners, and signage to be implemented. That message must be based in honesty, transparency, and compassion rather than appeasement and forged optimism
design goals
Sincerity
An earnest and public apology is foundational to rebuilding trust.
Empathy
Actively consider and address the human experiences within the system.
Participatory
Engage with the community to co-create solutions and shape the future together.
ideate

Restoring trust

Being the third largest private university in the united states, the example set by USC in taking an earnest approach in its communication strategy by empathizing with students will set the precedent for other institutions to follow.

We first explored themes that resonated with the student body while aligning with USC's brand. We primarily focused on developing the copy, crafting the messages that students need to hear next semester. Messages akin to “we’re all in this together” are not one of them.
implement

Fostering empathy

I conducted a redesign of USC’s Commitment to Change, launching the Student Petitions feature as the next step towards enhancing the school’s future.

A public platform for students to voice their concerns will help USC gain a better understanding of the student climate compared to memos. Students felt abandoned when their petitions were ignored, or when they recieved disingenuous statements from a website that reeks of a lack of effort. This is a tremendous opportunity for USC to make students feel heard, empowered, and supported.
conclusion
Long-term healing requires continuous work, not just a certain statement or action plan. We set forth to challenge and broaden our client's understanding of the problem.

This project is about sticking to my values as a designer to always advocate for the user, even if I face backlash. I also learned to zoom out of the brief and explore unconvential solutions, and was able to leverage my skills in product design to create meaningful change.
