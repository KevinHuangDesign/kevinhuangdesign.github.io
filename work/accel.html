overview
Current options for academic support is seen as inconvenient and unreliable, discouraging already busy college students from seeking help. Existing solutions for peer-tutoring are lackluster –– who has the time to swipe on tutors like Tinder when they're frustrated with a problem? Students feel the need to get help, often when it's too late. Thus, I set out to develop a prototype of a product that actually addresses the urgent academic needs of USC students.
challenge

What are we trying to solve?

How might we help college students get effective help while also balancing convenience and price?
project scope

Narrowing it down

As learning support can be a broad topic in which many aspects need to be accounted for, this project is focused on addressing the barriers to seeking help. Specifically, focusing on how a student can find help in the most efficient way while allowing them to study on their own time.

solution

How I approached the problem

Nearly all USC students live within 2 miles of campus, affording the ability to meet and study effortlessly. I wanted to resuscitate these interactions by creating the first solution that connects students to tutors who know the ropes at any time. The usage of an online marketplace for peer-to-peer tutoring empowers students to learn, work, and achieve their goals. 

Designing behavior

To make the experience of seeking help fulfilling, the habit-forming principles of Nir Eyal’s Hook Model is central to my strategy. The moment the trigger of feeling overwhelmed emerges, students receive personalized help promptly.

design goals
Empower
In times of stress, what matters most is that students feel reassured.
Reliable

Every action is supported by meaningful interactions.
“It’s like Uber, but for...”
Simply take a photo of your problem and choose from tutors ready to answer. Tutors can log on or off whenever they want.

How can I have tutors immediately answer customers in a convenient way for both parties?
Drawing from my DoorDash experience, tutors with the app opened have two minutes to view the problem and confirm they can answer. Having the power to choose your tutor fosters satisfaction, and motivates tutors to compete by confirming in a timely manner and being available.
‍
You're the boss
Although most students just need quick help with a problem or concept, those who want more can easily see a tutor’s availability and schedule a private lesson. Tutor enjoy the flexibility of choosing when, where, and how much they earn.

How might I design the best time picker for students to see availabilities and schedule a private lesson?
Through testing my designs with tutors, I learned most students want to schedule 1-hour lessons but some want longer. I designed the time picker so users can switch between dates quickly and find a time that works for their schedule.
Easy to get started
Students simply select the class they want help with and available tutors are shown. Privacy comes first, so everyone is on a first-name basis.

How might I develop the market, boost conversion rate, and encourage customer retention?
On-boarding is streamlined so students experience the value proposition before the hassle of creating an account. Micro-influencer marketing is leveraged to dismantle stigma and reframe tutoring as something "someone like me does." Community stories and useful articles provide continuous value.
