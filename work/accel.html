overview
Current options for academic support is seen as inconvenient and unreliable, discouraging already busy college students from seeking help. Students feel the need to get help, often when it's too late. Thus, I set out on my own to develop a prototype of a product that actually addresses the urgent academic needs of USC students.
challenge

What are we trying to solve?

How might we help college students get effective help while also balancing convenience and price?
project scope

Narrowing it down

As learning support can be a broad topic in which many aspects need to be accounted for, this project is focused on addressing the barriers to seeking help. Specifically, focusing on how a student can find help in the most efficient way while allowing them to study on their own time.

“Differences across professors is the hardest part in helping students. Sometimes none of us there have had that professor so we’re not really sure how to answer.”

“I was always too busy to attend review sessions so I usually study with my friends, but Zoom makes it awkward. The one time I did go right before midterms, I waited 30 minutes just to ask one question.” 

“Last semester I was so stressed studying for Finance. I wish I had somebody who could help because my friends weren’t able to.”

solution

How I approached the problem

Nearly all USC students live within 2 miles of campus, affording the ability to effortlessly meet and study with friends. But campus is now closed, and social distancing is in effect. Existing options for academic support have always failed to be viable because students procrastinate and only realize they need help right before the exam. There needs to be a better way. I set out to resuscitate these campus interactions by creating the first solution that connects students to tutors who know the ropes at any time.

Designing behavior

How might I change the experience of seeking help from being ineffective to fulfilling? I researched habit-forming principles of behavioral psychology to answer this question. I designed a way for students to get personalized help with their hardest classes – the moment they feel overwhelmed. The usage of an online marketplace for peer-to-peer tutoring empowers students to learn, work, and achieve their goals in the most efficient manner.

design goals
Empower
In times of stress, what matters most is that students feel reassured.
Reliable

“It’s like Uber, but for...”
When students are frustrated, they often use their phones to escape stressful feelings. Instead of just distracting them, what if that was the solution? What if you could just take a photo of what you're stuck with and a tutor solved your problem?

To provide problem help on-demand in a convenient way for both parties, I drew inspiration from my DoorDash experience. Tutors can log on or off whenever they want. When a request is made, they have two minutes to confirm they can answer. This system effectively develops new market segments; potential customers who didn't need a full hour lesson are more willing to get help, and tutors are motivated to compete by confirming in a timely manner and being available at the right time.
‍
You're the boss
Although most students just need prompt help with a problem, those who want more can easily see a tutor’s availability and schedule a private lesson. Tutor enjoy the flexibility of choosing when, where, and how much they earn.

How might I design the best time picker for students to see availabilities and schedule a private lesson? Testing my designs with tutors, I learned most students schedule 1-hour lessons. I designed the time picker to help users efficiently browse dates and book a time that works for their schedule.
Easy to get started
Students simply select the class they want help with and available tutors are shown. Privacy comes first, so everyone is on a first-name basis.

How might I develop the market, boost conversion rate, and encourage customer retention?
On-boarding is streamlined so students experience the value proposition before the hassle of creating an account. Collaborating with popular USC students leaverages micro-influencer to dismantle stigma, increase exchange value through social rewards, and reframe tutoring as "something someone like me does." By reliably alleviating the pain of frustrating concepts for students, opening Accel becomes a conditioned response that drives customer retention.
